Smoking

This traditional method involves burning dried cannabis flower in a joint, pipe, or bong. While it offers a rapid onset of effects, it can be harsh on the lungs due to the combustion process, potentially leading to respiratory issues.

Vaping

A more recent method that involves heating cannabis flower or concentrates to produce vapor, which is then inhaled. Vaping is considered less harmful than smoking as it avoids combustion, reducing the risk of lung damage.

Edibles

Cannabis-infused food or drinks, such as brownies, gummies, or tinctures. Edibles can take longer to take effect, often 30-90 minutes, but the effects can last for several hours. The delayed onset can be attributed to the digestive process.

Capsules

Cannabis is encapsulated in pill form for precise dosing and easier consumption. Capsules can offer a more consistent and predictable experience compared to edibles.

Topicals

Cannabis-infused creams, balms, or lotions applied directly to the skin for localized relief of pain, inflammation, or skin conditions. Topicals do not produce psychoactive effects as they are absorbed through the skin.

Tinctures

Liquid cannabis extracts that can be ingested orally or sublingually. Tinctures offer a flexible dosing option and can be added to food or drink. They provide a more rapid onset of effects compared to edibles.
