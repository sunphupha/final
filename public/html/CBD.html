What is CBD?

CBD, or cannabidiol, is a non-psychoactive compound found in cannabis. Unlike THC, CBD does not produce a "high" but offers various therapeutic benefits, making it a popular choice for medicinal use.

Research suggests that CBD may help with conditions such as anxiety, chronic pain, inflammation, and even epilepsy. It works by interacting with the body's endocannabinoid system, which regulates processes like mood, pain sensation, and immune response.

CBD products come in many forms, including oils, edibles, creams, and capsules. Always check the product label for dosage and consult with a healthcare professional for proper usage, especially if you're new to CBD.
