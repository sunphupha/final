What is THC?

THC, or tetrahydrocannabinol, is the main psychoactive compound in cannabis. It's responsible for the "high" that many people associate with marijuana use. THC interacts with receptors in the brain to produce various effects, such as euphoria, relaxation, and heightened sensory perception.

THC levels can vary significantly between different cannabis strains, typically ranging from 5% to 30%. Higher THC percentages often correlate with stronger psychoactive effects. Understanding THC levels is essential for choosing the right cannabis product for your needs.

While THC offers many potential benefits, such as pain relief and improved mood, it may also cause side effects like anxiety or dizziness, especially for first-time users. Always start with a low dose and consult a professional if you're new to cannabis.
